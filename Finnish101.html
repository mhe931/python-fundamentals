Important Pronunciation Notes

üí° Tip: Pronunciation Consistency

  • One Sound Per Letter: This is your superpower in Finnish! Every letter is always pronounced the same way. What you see is what you say.
  • Double Letters are Long: Double consonants (kk, pp, tt) and double vowels (aa, oo, yy) are held **twice as long**. This is crucial, as it changes meaning: tuli (fire) vs. tuuli (wind).
  • Primary Stress: Stress *always* falls on the **first syllable** of the word. Keep it simple!

The Partitive Case (Partitiivi) in Detail

üéØ Four Core Uses (A1.1 Level)

  • 1. **Negation:** The object of a negative sentence is always in the Partitive. (En osta kirjaa. - I don't buy a book.)
  • 2. **Counting:** Used with numbers 2 or higher. (Kolme omenaa. - Three apples.)
  • 3. **Uncountable/Mass Nouns:** Nouns that represent an unspecified amount. (Juon vett√§. - I drink water.)
  • 4. **Unfinished Action:** Actions that are ongoing or incomplete. (Luen kirjaa. - I am reading a book [not finished].)
