Partitiivi (The Big Three Rules) üìê

The object of a negative sentence is always in the Partitive.

KPT is Logic!

KPT (Consonant Gradation) isn't random; it's a linguistic efficiency tool. It evolved because early Finnish speakers tried to avoid having too many short, hard syllables in a row.

It helps Finns instantly tell if a word is the simple base form (Strong Grade) or if it has an ending attached (Weak Grade). It's a fundamental part of the language's "operating system."

Agglutination is Fun!

Finnish is an agglutinative language. This means you "glue" suffixes onto words to change their meaning, instead of using many small words (like 'in', 'my', 'from').

One word can be a whole sentence:

  • Talossani (in my house)
