Pillar 1: Paragraph & Structure

The T-E-A Method (The Simple Academic Rule)

Every single paragraph in your exam should follow this formula to ensure clarity and coherence:

  • T - Topic Sentence: The main idea of the paragraph. It must directly support your overall thesis.
  • E - Evidence: The **paraphrased data** or citation that proves your point (e.g., the 91.3% data point).
  • A - Analysis: Your explanation. Why does this evidence matter? This is your *voice* and *critique*.

Paraphrasing vs. Quoting

Always paraphrase and synthesize the ideas unless the exact words are critical. This shows the examiner you understand the source.

  • Paraphrase (Preferred): Explain the idea in your own simple, clear words.
  • Direct Quote (Limited): Use sparingly. Must be exact and require a page number.

Pillar 2: Citation Compliance

APA In-Text Citation (The Mandatory Format)

Your citation must include the author, year, and **page number** if it is a direct quote or a very specific finding:

[Paraphrased Idea] (Author, Year).
The AI focus is too narrow (Alam et al., 2022).
[Direct Quote] "..." (Author, Year, p. Page Number).
Visuals are "processed differently" (Weikmann & Lecheler, 2023, p. 2).

Appendix Mandate (CRITICAL)

The Appendix **must** contain the direct quote for every single in-text citation you use. Do not forget the author, year, and the exact quote.

Example Appendix Entry (Must be Exact):

1. Alam, F., et al. (2022). A survey on multimodal disinformation detection.

Pillar 3: Argumentation Models

Toulminian System (Logic & Directness)

  • **Goal:** To prove your point directly using evidence. Very useful for **technical critiques**.
  • Structure: Start with your **Claim**, then give **Data** to support it, and explain the **Warrant** (why the data proves the claim).
  • Example: Claim: AI is unprepared. Data: 91.3% data point. Warrant: That gap means it's blind to the threat.

Rogerian System (Empathy & Cooperation)

  • **Goal:** To reach common ground by acknowledging the opposition first. Ideal for **proposing evolution/solutions**.
  • Structure: Acknowledge **Opposing View/Validity** (TSE is good), state your **Position** (methods must evolve), and explain **Benefits** (this evolution keeps the science relevant).
  • Example: Acknowledge TSE is the gold standard, THEN argue current methods fail that standard.

SOURCE I: ALAM ET AL. (The AI Blind Spot)

The Central Claim: Multimodality vs. Text-Only Models

  • The Threat: **Multimodality** (Text, Images, Video, Speech) makes disinformation easier to consume and spread (p. 1).
  • The Data: **91.3%** of fake news detection models rely primarily on **Text**, ignoring images/video (p. 2). This is the key statistic to use as evidence.
  • The Deficiency: The two core parts of disinformation—**Factuality** (is it true?) and **Harmfulness** (is the intent to harm?)—are studied in isolation, which is a major mistake (p. 1).

Future Directions (Your Recommendations)

To fix the flaw, AI research must devise a single **framework** that combines all signals and addresses both **factuality and harmfulness** simultaneously (p. 9). We need transparent models that can explain *why* something is fake, not just flag it.

SOURCE II: WEIKMANN & LECHELER (Visual Power)

Psychological Mechanisms of Deception

  • Core Power: Visuals are **processed differently** than text, creating **"rich sensory responses"** that stick in memory and are perceived as more credible (p. 2).
  • The Trick: The **realism heuristic** is the belief that rich media (images/video) must be real, leading to **faulty processing** where users fail to question their authenticity (p. 7).
  • The Range: Disinformation ranges from **low sophistication** (e.g., decontextualization, using a real photo with a fake caption) to **high sophistication** (e.g., deepfakes) (p. 3).

The Final Systemic Consequence

The **Liar's Dividend:** The existence of deepfakes confuses citizens so much that they **"mistake real for fake"** (p. 11), leading to a long-term **loss of trust** in all media and institutions.

MODEL ESSAY: PART 1/2

Rogerian Argument: Traditional Survey Flaws

[P1: Acknowledging Validity] Our core belief is that traditional survey and polling methods are valuable because they gave us the gold standard of research. This standard is the **Total Survey Error (TSE)** framework, which correctly defines the goal of all data collection: to capture the "true value" of public opinion. Researchers widely respect this framework because it systematically shows that data quality is threatened by errors at every stage, from design to execution (Groves & Lyberg, 2010, p. 850). Acknowledging this comprehensive approach is the starting point for all good research.

[P2: Stating Position and Context] However, respecting the TSE framework means we must be honest about its practical failure in the modern world. The belief that traditional surveys can achieve this "true value" is no longer valid. In a complex society, their rigid, step-by-step methodology is simply too slow and clumsy to capture the fluid nature of human opinions. Since the TSE framework defines an error as *any deviation* from the truth (Groves & Lyberg, 2010), the inflexible design of traditional methods multiplies these small errors at every stage. They cannot control for complex factors like rapidly changing social influence, meaning they ultimately fail to meet their own highly respected standard of accuracy.

Model Essay: Conclusion & Rationale

[P3: Statement of Benefits] The benefit of this critique is that it directs us toward a solution that actually fulfills the promise of the TSE framework. We should not throw away the science, but rather adopt new, multi-modal methods designed for the fluid, interconnected nature of truth today. The benefit of dynamic data collection systems (like those that use digital network data) is that they can minimize the error in both the **measurement process** and the **representation process**, creating data that is closer to the essential "true value** than any old telephone poll ever could. This shift is not an attack on the past; it is the necessary evolution to ensure the fundamental principles of survey science remain relevant and accurate in the 21st century.

Argumentation System Rationale (100 Words)

For this paper, I used the Rogerian system. I chose this because the goal was not to attack traditional methods, but to show that we understand and respect the foundation of their work—the Total Survey Error (TSE) concept. By first validating the TSE framework, the paper avoids confrontation and makes the final argument (that old methods fail the framework) much more cooperative and convincing. It's a great approach for making serious critiques in an academic setting.
