L1: Data Architecture - The Blueprint

DA Definition & Core Focus

  • DA is the **Framework for managing data assets**, defining structures, integration, storage, movement, and governance.
  • Answers the question: "How should data be **organized, connected, and secured**?"
  • Focuses on structure, standards, and **long-term scalability** of enterprise data.
**Prerequisites:** Basic knowledge of databases and data engineering principles (ICAT1140, ICAT2190).

Roles & Overlap: DA, DE, DS

RolePrimary FocusOutput/Goal
**DA Architect****Blueprint**, Structure, GovernanceDefines **Standards** and Models.
**DE Engineer****Implementation**, Pipelines (ETL/ELT)Moves, transforms, and **stores data efficiently**.
**DS Scientist****Consumption**, Insights, MLGenerates **predictions** and business value.
**Overlap:** DA & DE choose the schema/storage. DE & DS optimize pipelines for ML/feature stores.

DA Philosophies & Evolution

  • **Form Follows Function:** Design (form) must be driven by business needs (function), not technical fads. (e.g., Regulatory Reporting $\rightarrow$ Auditable, Versioned Data).
  • **Evolution (Historical):**
    • 1970s: **Relational Model** (Schema-on-Write).
    • 1980s: **Data Warehouses**.
    • 2010s: **Cloud Data Lakes**.
    • 2020s: **Data Mesh, Lakehouse**. (Solving previous scalability/flexibility limits).

The Three Conceptual Levels (TOGAF)

LevelFocusArtifacts
**1. Conceptual**Business relevance (What data matters).High-level Entity Relationships, Data Domains.
**2. Logical**Detailed structure (Entities, Attributes, Keys).Logical Data Models (Database Agnostic).
**3. Physical**Technology implementation.Physical Schema (Tables, Indexes, Data Types).

What is NOT Data Architecture

  • A list of tools (**technology stack**) is not an architecture ("We use Snowflake and Power BI").
  • An **ETL workflow** alone is not architecture; it's part of implementation.
  • A set of diagrams **without principles or governance** is not architecture.
  • **Ad-hoc solutions** lacking enterprise standardization.

L2: Foundational Principles

Core Architectural Principles

  • **Scalability, Extensibility, Flexibility:** Design for growth in volume, velocity, and variety.
  • **Modularity:** Use reusable, interchangeable components (e.g., microservices, adapters).
  • **Simplicity:** Prefer clear, modular designs over complex, bespoke solutions.
  • **Interoperability:** Use open standards/protocols (APIs, open document formats) for integration.

Trust, Quality, and Ownership

  • **Single Source of Truth (SSOT):** One authoritative source for each data element (e.g., CRM for customer master data).
  • **Data as a Product:** Data is managed with a lifecycle, roadmap, and SLAs for utility and reliability.
  • **Metadata Management:** Metadata (context, lineage, discoverability) is crucial for organization (e.g., the photo timestamp that clears a suspect's alibi).
  • **Data Stewardship:** Assigning **ownership and accountability** for every dataset (e.g., CRM Service Manager decides data propagation).

Scalability & Loose Coupling (The Problem)

  • **Scalability Issue:** Request-reply architecture (e.g., a batch job polling for stock prices) does not scale because many clients hitting the source system causes overload and bottlenecks.
  • **Problematic Components:** Non-elastic infrastructure, database bottlenecks (too many rows/users), lack of caching.
**Solution:** Use **Elastic Infrastructure** (Cloud scaling) and **Distributed Processing** (Kafka/Spark).

Scalability & Loose Coupling (The Solution)

  • **Solution:** Use a **Push-Based Broker (Pub-Sub)** strategy.
    • The Stock Exchange System publishes `publishStockPriceList` once to a **Broker**.
    • The Broker handles the fan-out to all subscribed clients (News Sites).
    • The core system is **decoupled** (Loose Coupling) and **not loaded** by the number of clients.
  • **Modularity (Adapter):** Interchangeable software components (adapters) allow a client to connect via different protocols (HTTP vs. FTP) without changing the core business logic.

Governance, Quality & Security

  • **Data Quality by Design:** Embed quality checks (e.g., `loan_date < return_date`) into pipelines.
  • **Privacy & Compliance:** Adherence to regulations (GDPR, HIPAA) via PII masking, encryption (at rest/in transit).
  • **Data Integrity:** Maintaining accuracy and consistency throughout the lifecycle.
  • **Roles:** **Data Owners** (authority over data access) and **Data Stewards** (daily quality, consistency).

L3: Data Warehousing - The OLAP Core

Definition & OLAP

  • A relational database that integrates copies of transaction data from OLTP systems for **analytical use (OLAP)**.
  • **OLAP:** Online Analytical Processing. Uses complex queries on aggregated historical data.
  • **Data is cleaned, enriched, and transformed** to act as the "single source of truth."
  • **Schema:** **Schema-on-Write** (defined in advance for fast SQL queries).

ETL Process & The Staging Area

  • **ETL (Extract, Transform, Load):** The process used to populate a DWH.
  • **Staging Layer (Area):** A temporary workspace where:
    • Data is **Consolidated** and **Enriched**.
    • **Cleaning** (fixes errors, removes duplicates) and **Validation** happen.
    • **Sensitive information** is removed/masked before loading to the DWH.
  • **Load Types:** **Full Load** (easy, inefficient) or **Incremental Batch Load** (requires timestamp for auditing, faster).

DWH Modeling Approaches

  • **Goal:** Denormalize data for fast query response times and ease of use (fewer joins).
  • **Star Schema:** Central **Fact Table** (measures) referencing Dimension Tables (context). **Dimensions are not dependent** on each other (flat structure).
  • **Snowflake Schema:** Normalizes dimension tables into sub-dimensions (more joins, higher normalization).
  • **Galaxy Schema:** Multiple Fact Tables sharing dimension tables.

DWH Tiers & Components

  • **Top Tier:** **Front-end Clients** (Reporting, Data Mining, BI/Analytics).
  • **Middle Tier:** **Analytics Engine** (Used to access and analyze data).
  • **Bottom Tier:** **Database Server** (Stores data).
  • **Storage:** Frequently accessed data $\rightarrow$ **Fast storage (SSD)**; Infrequently accessed $\rightarrow$ **Cheap object store (S3/ADLS)**.
DWHs answer Descriptive (What happened), Diagnostic (Why), Predictive (What will happen), and Prescriptive (What to do) questions.

OLAP Operations: Drill & Roll

OperationActionResult
**Roll-Up**Aggregates data by reducing dimensions (e.g., sum sales).$\uparrow$ **Less Detail** (City $\rightarrow$ Country)
**Drill-Down**Navigates to a more detailed level (reverse of roll-up).$\downarrow$ **More Detail** (Quarter $\rightarrow$ Month)

OLAP Operations: Slice & Dice

  • **Slice:** Filters the data cube by fixing **one dimension** to a single value (e.g., `WHERE year = 2024`). Results in a sub-cube with one less dimension.
  • **Dice:** Filters the data cube based on values of **two or more dimensions** (e.g., `WHERE Region IN ('NA', 'EU') AND QTR IN ('Q1', 'Q2')`). The dimension remains in the analysis if multiple options are restricted.

L4: Data Lakes & Modern DW

Why Data Lakes (DL) Emerged

  • **DWH Limitation:** Rigid **Schema-on-Write** makes adding new data types (logs, IoT, unstructured) difficult and slow.
  • DL provides a flexible, low-cost repository for **all types of data** (Structured, Semi-structured, Unstructured).
  • **Data Lake Principle:** **Schema-on-Read** (structure defined only when queried).
  • **Process:** **ELT (Extract, Load, Transform)** is preferred, as data is loaded raw first.

DL Design & The Data Swamp

  • **Bottom-Up Design:** Start with **Ingestion** and **Storage** before modeling. (Ingest $\rightarrow$ Analyze $\rightarrow$ Structure).
  • **Key Enabler:** Cloud separation of **Storage and Compute**.
  • **Data Swamp:** The common pitfall where chaotic, undocumented data (missing metadata/schema) makes the lake untrustworthy and unusable.

Practical Design: Layered Zones

DL Layered Architecture (Zones)

  • **Raw Layer:** Holds unprocessed data (append-only) for traceability.
  • **Cleaned/Conformed Layer:** Data standardized (e.g., Parquet), validated, and enriched.
  • **Presentation Layer:** Curated, business-ready datasets optimized for consumption (BI/ML).
  • **Sandbox:** Safe, experimental workspace for data science exploration.

The Data Lakehouse

  • **Definition:** A unified data architecture combining the **flexibility of the Data Lake** with the **reliability of the Data Warehouse**.
  • **Built on:** Cloud object storage (ADLS, S3) + **open table formats** (Delta Lake, Apache Hudi).
  • **DWH Features Added:** **ACID Transactions** (Atomicity, Consistency, Isolation, Durability), **Schema Enforcement**, and integrated governance.
  • **Advantage:** Supports both BI/Reporting and advanced ML/AI pipelines on a single copy of data.

Right-Sizing (Small Org Case Study)

  • **Principle:** **Right-Sizing is Critical.** More expensive $\neq$ better.
  • **120GB Case:** Enterprise platforms (Snowflake, Databricks) are **overkill** and too costly for $\lt$1TB of structured data.
  • **Recommendation:** Start simple. A lightweight modern stack ($\text{Cloud Postgres} + \text{ADF} + \text{dbt} + \text{PowerBI}$) balances modern practices with reasonable cost and scales easily up to $\text{10TB}+$.
