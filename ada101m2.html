Lecture 1: DA - The Blueprint

Definition & Core Focus

  • DA is the Framework for managing data assets (structure, integration, movement, governance).
  • Answers: "How should data be organized, connected, and secured?"
  • Focus: structure, standards, and long-term scalability.

DA is the Blueprint; DE is the builder; DS is the consumer.

L1: Roles & Philosophies

Key Principle: Form Follows Function

Design (form) must be driven by the actual business needs (function).

Example:

  • Function: Real-time fraud detection → Form: Streaming pipeline.
  • Function: Regulatory reporting → Form: Auditable, versioned models.

L1: Schema Types & Evolution

Models and History

  • Schema-on-Write: Schema fixed before data is written (DWH, Rigid).
  • Schema-on-Read: Structure defined when data is queried (Data Lake, Flexible).
  • Modeling Levels: Conceptual → Logical → Physical.
  • Evolution: Relational (70s) → DWH (80s) → Big Data (00s) → Lakehouse (20s).

Lecture 2: Core Principles

Business Alignment & Agnosticism

  • Business Alignment: Must serve business strategy.
  • Technology Agnosticism: Design independent of specific products.
  • Modularity: Use reusable, independent components.
  • Loose Coupling: Systems connect via well-defined interfaces.

L2: Trust & Ownership

SSOT, Data as a Product & Metadata

  • Single Source of Truth (SSOT): One authoritative source.
  • Data as a Product: Explicit ownership, quality metrics.
  • Metadata: Data about data. Crucial for lineage.
  • Data Stewardship: Assigns ownership.

L2: Scalability & Frameworks

Scaling & Organization

  • Scalability: Push-Based Broker (Pub-Sub) pattern.
  • TOGAF: Process-oriented roadmap.
  • Zachman: Taxonomy-oriented matrix.

Lecture 3: DWH - Structured Core

Definition & ETL Flow

  • DWH is for OLAP on structured historical data.
  • Process: ETL (Transform before Load).
  • Staging Layer: Temporary workspace.
  • Technology: Columnar Databases.

L3: Modeling & Loading

Schemas & Flow Types

  • Schema-on-Write: Fixed schema.
  • Goal: Denormalization for speed.
  • Star Schema: Fact → Dimension tables.
  • Loading: Incremental Batch Load.

Lecture 4: Data Lakes

Flexible Storage & Risk

  • Purpose: All data types.
  • Schema-on-Read: Flexible.
  • Design: Bottom-Up.
  • Risk: Data Swamp without governance.

L4: Zones & ADLS Gen2

Structure & Technology

  • Zones: Raw → Cleaned → Curated.
  • ADLS Gen2: HDFS-compatible.
  • Hierarchical Namespace for speed.
  • ACLS + RBAC security.

L4: The Data Lakehouse

The Modern Convergence

  • Definition: DL + DWH reliability.
  • ACID Transactions on lake storage.
  • Unified BI + ML.
  • Right-Sizing Critical.
