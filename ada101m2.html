Lecture 1: DA - The Blueprint

Definition & Core Focus

  • DA is the Framework for managing data assets (structure, integration, movement, governance).
  • Answers: "How should data be organized, connected, and secured?"
  • Focus: structure, standards, and long-term scalability.

DA is the Blueprint; DE is the builder; DS is the consumer.

L1: Roles & Philosophies

Key Principle: Form Follows Function

Design (form) must be driven by the actual business needs (function).

Example:

  • Function: Real-time fraud detection $\rightarrow$ Form: Streaming pipeline.
  • Function: Regulatory reporting $\rightarrow$ Form: Auditable, versioned models.

L1: Schema Types & Evolution

Models and History

  • Schema-on-Write: Schema fixed before data is written (DWH, Rigid).
  • Schema-on-Read: Structure defined when data is queried (Data Lake, Flexible).
  • Modeling Levels: Conceptual $\rightarrow$ Logical $\rightarrow$ Physical.
  • Evolution: Relational (70s) $\rightarrow$ DWH (80s) $\rightarrow$ Big Data (00s) $\rightarrow$ Lakehouse (20s).

Lecture 2: Core Principles

Business Alignment & Agnosticism

  • Business Alignment: Must serve business strategy.
  • Technology Agnosticism: Design independent of specific products.
  • Modularity: Use reusable, independent components (Adapters, Microservices).
  • Loose Coupling: Systems connect via well-defined interfaces (APIs, canonical models).

L2: Trust & Ownership

SSOT, Data as a Product & Metadata

  • Single Source of Truth (SSOT): One authoritative source for each data element (ensures consistency).
  • Data as a Product: Data is managed with explicit ownership, quality metrics, and measured utility.
  • Metadata: Data about data (schema, lineage). Crucial for context and auditability.
  • Data Stewardship: Assigns ownership and accountability (managing privacy compliance).

L2: Scalability & Frameworks

Scaling & Organization

  • Scalability Solution: Use the Push-Based Broker (Pub-Sub) pattern to decouple sources from clients.
  • TOGAF: Process-oriented roadmap for *how* to develop architecture.
  • Zachman: Taxonomy/Schema-oriented matrix for *what* artifacts to document.

Lecture 3: DWH - Structured Core

Definition & ETL Flow

  • DWH is for OLAP on structured historical data.
  • Process: ETL (Extract, Transform, Load) is used (Transform happens before Load).
  • Staging Layer: Temporary workspace for 'T' step (Cleaning, Validation, sensitive data removal).
  • Technology: Often Columnar Databases (optimized for fast aggregation/read).

L3: Modeling & Loading

Schemas & Flow Types

  • Schema-on-Write: Must be fixed (rigid).
  • Goal: Denormalization for fast query times.
  • Star Schema: Central Fact Table $\rightarrow$ flat Dimension Tables.
  • Loading: Full Load (inefficient) vs. Incremental Batch Load (faster, requires timestamps).

Lecture 4: Data Lakes

Flexible Storage & Risk

  • Purpose: Handle all data types (Structured, Semi, Unstructured).
  • Schema-on-Read: Structure defined when consumed (flexible).
  • Design: Bottom-Up (Ingest $\rightarrow$ Analyze $\rightarrow$ Structure).
  • Risk: A poor lake becomes a Data Swamp (lacks metadata/governance).

L4: Zones & ADLS Gen2

Structure & Technology

  • Zones: Raw $\rightarrow$ Cleaned $\rightarrow$ Presentation/Curated (to prevent swamps).
  • ADLS Gen2: Azure's HDFS-compatible lake.
  • Key Feature: Hierarchical Namespace (enables fast folder ops like rename, crucial for big data).
  • Security: ACLS and RBAC.

L4: The Data Lakehouse

The Modern Convergence

  • Definition: Built on the Data Lake (DL) but adds DWH reliability.
  • Key Feature: Enables ACID Transactions and Schema Enforcement on DL storage.
  • Benefit: Unified platform for both reliable SQL/BI and flexible ML/AI.
  • Principle: Right-Sizing is Critical (don't use Lakehouse for small, structured data).
