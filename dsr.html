Mobile Health App Design

A DSR Approach to Remote Chronic Disease Management

A Design Science Research Proposal

1. WHAT are we doing? (The Artifact & Problem)

The Solution

Design a **Mobile Medical App** (A DSR Method/Artifact)

The Need

  • Stakeholders: **Patients** (Chronic diseases like diabetics, hypertension) & **Health Professionals**.
  • Purpose: Enable **remote monitoring** and **consultations**.

The Problem

**Lack of regular access** to integrated medical, nutrition, mental, and physical care from home.

Context: Private individuals and medical professionals in **Iran** and **Nigeria**.

2. WHY are we doing it? (Significance & Gaps)

Problem Significance

Patients lack **integrated medical and lifestyle** support. No consistent monitoring, especially for those who live alone.

Central Requirements

Patients

  • Easy Health Tracking
  • Timely Consultations
  • Personalized Advice

Professionals

  • Efficient Patient Monitoring
  • Access to Accurate Data
  • Remote Advice Capability

The Gap

Existing solutions are often **single-disease focused**, lack **multi-disciplinary integration** (medical/nutrition/mental health), and are **too complicated** to use widely.

3. HOW are we doing it? (The Solution Mechanism)

Fundamental Mechanism

A **Mobile Health Application** integrating Telemedicine, Self-Monitoring, and Professional Consultation into one platform.

Core Insight

**Regular remote monitoring** and timely **multi-specialist feedback** significantly improves patient engagement and reduces complications.

Concrete Components

  • Secure User Profiles & Health Logs
  • Daily Vital Sign & Symptom Monitoring
  • **Communication Tools** (Text, Voice, Video)
  • Personalized Lifestyle Recommendations
  • Alerts for Abnormal Readings

4. HOW are we verifying it? (Validation & Future)

Demonstration & Utility

  • **Demonstrate:** Develop a **Prototype** and conduct a **Pilot Study** (e.g., with Diabetes/Hypertension patients).
  • **Evaluate:** Measure **User Satisfaction**, **Adherence** to advice, and **Reduction in Unnecessary Hospital Visits**.

Enhancements (Based on Initial Feedback)

  • Add **AI-based Predictive Analytics** to identify early warning signs.
  • Improve UI/UX for older, less tech-savvy users.
  • Localize content and language for **Iran** and **Nigeria**.
  • Integrate with **Wearable Devices** (IoT).

5. WHAT will we LEARN? (Impact & Limitations)

Research Contribution

  • Insights on designing **multi-disciplinary telemedicine** for chronic care.
  • Framework for digital health solutions in **developing-country contexts** (Iran, Nigeria).

Key Limitations

  • **Limited Pilot Scope** (Sample size, short duration).
  • Potential technical challenges (**connectivity**, data privacy).
  • **User adoption** barriers (especially among the elderly).

Future Research

Conduct **longitudinal studies**, explore **AI-driven personalization**, and examine **policy/ethical** aspects for national implementation.
